About Coral Bleaching

Coral bleaching occurs when corals are stressed by changes in conditions such as temperature, light, or nutrients. One of the most significant stressors is rising sea temperatures.

TSA_DHW (Thermal Stress Anomaly - Degree Heating Weeks) measures accumulated heat stress over 12 weeks. Higher TSA_DHW values correlate with increased coral bleaching.

When water temperatures rise even 1–2°C above the normal maximum, and persist, it can lead to widespread bleaching events.

When coral bleaching occurs, it leads to a decline in biodiversity, impacts fish populations, disrupts food webs, and reduces the structural complexity of coral reefs, leaving them more vulnerable to other stresses.
